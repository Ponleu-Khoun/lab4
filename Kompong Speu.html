Kompong Speu

Kompong Speu province is a province of Cambodia. It borders the provinces of Pursat and Kampong Chhnang to the north, Kandal to the east, Tak√©o to the southeast, and Koh Kong to the southwest. The province has a population of around 716,517 people, with a mix of Khmer, Vietnamese, Chinese, and Cham ethnic groups. [1] The capital of Kompong Speu province is Chbar Mon, which is located about 48 kilometers west of Phnom Penh. The province is divided into 8 districts, including Basedth, Chbar Mon, Kong Pisei, Aoral, Oudong, Phnom Sruoch, Samraong Tong, and Thpong. [1] Kompong Speu province is known for its rich agricultural land, with rice, corn, and rubber being major crops. The region is also home to several historic sites, including the ancient temple of Phnom Chiso, which dates back to the 11th century. [2]
