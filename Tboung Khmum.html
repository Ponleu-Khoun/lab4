Tboung Khmum

Tboung Khmum Province, located in the eastern part of Cambodia, is a destination brimming with cultural richness, historical significance, and natural beauty. Established in 2013, it is one of the newest provinces in Cambodia, carved out of the eastern part of Kampong Cham Province. Named after the Khmer word for “east”, Tboung Khmum is often referred to as Tboung Khmum Municipality. This province is known for its lush landscapes, serene countryside, and the Mekong River, which flows along its western border. With its blend of historical sites, rural charm, and cultural experiences, Tboung Khmum offers visitors a unique perspective on Cambodia’s past and present.
